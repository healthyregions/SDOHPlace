This spring, the SDOH & Place Project released a new round of Fellow Showcases highlighting how geospatial storytelling can deepen our understanding of health, equity, and place. Developed by ambassadors of the 2025 Fellowship cohort, each project combines spatial data, public health research, and interactive web mapping to explore social and environmental issues across the United States.

The 2025 cohort demonstrates the breadth and versatility of SDOH-informed geospatial visualization. This year's projects examine issues ranging from mapping Hepatitis C service sites in Los Angeles and the role of social infrastructure in spatial equity in Denver, to an overview of community health throughout the state of Illinois.

Further details on each individual project, including tech specs, motivations behind the development, and links to the application itself, can be found on our Showcases page: [sdohplace.org/showcase](https://sdohplace.org/showcase).
