* Wednesday, September 23rd - Friday, September 25th
* The Chicago Loop, Illinois 

## Overview

The [SDOH & Place Project](https://sdohplace.org/project) works to build a community of practice around defining & using community-level social determinants of health (SDOH). We are accomplishing this through discussions on defining SDOH and using SDOH data, engaging new types of collaborations and applications with design justice, and breaking ground in developing new types of infrastructures & tools to support this work.

At the **2026 SDOH & Place Symposium**, our growing community will come together to learn about current health equity initiatives and research, break bread, celebrate accomplishments, and build new connections.

## Propose a Talk or Apply to Attend In-Person

Each year, we invite our SDOH & Place Community of Practice to join us in Chicago, and open applications for  professionals working within the realm of SDOH and place to submit their proposal to attend. **If you would like to receive an invite to attend this year's Symposium**, you can submit a talk proposal or apply to attend as a guest. 

There is no symposium registration cost. For accepted applicants, food will be provided at the conference at no additional cost. 

To keep attendance free, however, **registration is limited due to space availability**. 

All sessions will be live streamed and recorded for future sharing.

All applicants should include a proposal for a talk or reason for attending (250 words MAX). **Talk proposals will be given special consideration, and priority for travel funding over, guest applications.** 

Deadline to submit your application is **11:59 PM CT on Friday, July 31st, or when spots are filled.** All applicants will be notified of their application on a rolling basis. 

Lodging and travel to Chicago will be the guest's responsibility and will not be covered by the event organizers. 

**A select number of travel awards may be made available to offset cost**; please indicate your interest when completing the application. Travel awards will be given out to select applicants ***after*** the Symposium. 

If you have any questions, concerns, or run into any issues please contact our Research Coordinator, Marc Astacio-Palmer (mastacio@illinois.edu).

### How to Propose a Talk or Apply to Attend*

Anyone, including already invited-guests, may propose a talk to present at the Symposium. 

Anyone interested in attending the Symposium, but have not yet received an invitation, may submit a talk proposal, or apply to attend as a guest. Talk proposals will receive special consideration and priority for travel funding over attendance applications, even if your talk is not accepted into the official program. 

1. Provide a copy of your Resume/CV in your application 
2. **For Talk Proposals:** Include a working title and abstract of your proposed talk (250 words MAX)
3. **For Attendee Applicants:** Provide a reason for attending (250 words MAX)
4. If you are invited to attend, you must confirm your RSVP within 2 weeks of attending, or your spot will be offered to another applicant 

**Submit your talk proposal, or application for attendance by completing this form:** [go.illinois.edu/26-SYMPOSIUM-APPLY.](https://go.illinois.edu/26-SYMPOSIUM-APPLY)

**This form will close at 11:59 PM CT on Friday, July 31st, or when spots are filled.** 

\*If you are interested in applying for a Travel Award, please make sure you are aware of your immigration status if you are a non-US Citizen. The list of approved immigration status' can be found [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1dF3vmrw9x1TjkkUSbFWuWGiTkOStzX7Sv3p6V3qoYxo/edit?usp=sharing).
